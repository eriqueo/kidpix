# Kid Pix 2 — User's Guide (Macintosh)

> **Provenance / why this file exists.** This is a cleaned-up transcription of the
> original **Kid Pix 2 User's Guide for Macintosh** (Brøderbund, © 1994), OCR'd from
> the scan archived at the Internet Archive
> ([`kidpix2usersguidemacintosh_2940110x`](https://archive.org/details/kidpix2usersguidemacintosh_2940110x)).
> It is kept in this repo as the **authoritative reference for original tool/effect
> behavior** — especially the Electric Mixer effects (The Outliner, Zoom In, Broken
> Glass, The Highlighter, Wrap Around, etc.) that we are re-implementing faithfully in
> the modular JS engine. When in doubt about what an effect "should" do, this manual is
> the source of truth for intent.
>
> OCR noise (the kids'-artwork gallery pages and a few illegible fragments) has been
> omitted; substantive text — particularly every tool and effect description — is
> preserved as faithfully as the scan allows. Page numbers from the print edition are
> noted where useful.

## Special Features

**No one's too young... No one's too old... for Kid Pix 2!**

All kids are artists. Give them a box of crayons or a set of finger paints and they
plunge right in, not worried about making messes or doing things "right." For them, art
and play are inseparable; the process is as important as the result. Now at last,
there's a program that takes advantage of kids' natural creative impulses and lets them
use the computer in a playful way to create art that's uniquely their own.

Kid Pix 2 is a paint and picture show program designed just for kids. It was created by
a dad — Craig Hickman — for his three-year-old son Ben. Craig decided to solve the
problems his son had with "big" paint programs by writing a paint program just for kids.

Brøderbund's Kid Pix 2 combines two great products: **Kid Pix** and **Kid Pix
Companion**. It's loaded with fun features — crazy sound effects, patterns that burst
into color, brushes that paint in wacky ways, and **SlideShow**, a tool for making
multimedia presentations with Kid Pix 2 pictures. It retains the natural, easy-to-use,
kid-proof features Craig built into the program, including:

- A **Small Kids Mode** that turns off the menu bar so young children can't open menus
  and make selections accidentally.
- A drawing area visible all at once on screen. No scroll bars. What you see is what you
  print.
- Tools and tool options available right on the screen.
- Text and numbers that can be added to pictures without the keyboard. The program
  speaks the names of letters and numbers out loud as they're selected — in English or
  Spanish.
- Sound and visual effects that add fun while stimulating creativity and reinforcing
  learning.
- A menu of extra creativity starters.

## Working with Kid Pix 2

Starting Kid Pix 2 is like entering your very own artist's studio. You draw by first
selecting a tool and tool options, then holding the mouse button down and moving the
mouse. Nothing is hidden from view; when you print, everything in the drawing area
appears on the page.

### The Kid Pix 2 Drawing Screen

- **Menu Bar** — File, Edit, Goodies, Switcheroo (at the top of the screen).
- **Tools** (down the left side):
  - Wacky Pencil
  - Line
  - Rectangle
  - Oval
  - Wacky Brush
  - Electric Mixer
  - Paint Can
  - Eraser
  - Text
  - Rubber Stamps
  - Moving Van
  - The Undo Guy
- **Current Color / Color Palette** (left, beneath the tools, on color systems).
- **Tool Options** (along the bottom; change depending on the selected tool).

### Menus

Menus on the menu bar let you edit, save, and print your work. The Goodies and
Switcheroo menus provide access to special features. To open a menu and choose a
command, point to the menu, press the mouse button, move the pointer down to the
command, and release.

### Tools

Each tool has its own set of options displayed at the bottom of the screen. The pointer
changes shape depending on the tool selected. **To select a tool, point to it and click.**
The selected tool is highlighted and its options displayed.

### Tool Options

The options vary the basic behavior of the tool (line width, pattern, drawing style,
erase style, etc.). **To select a tool option, point to it and click.** A **▶ icon** at
the end of a set of options indicates more options are available — click it to see them.

### Color

Kid Pix 2 uses the full range of color available on your Mac. On a color system, a color
palette appears at the left beneath the tools (monochrome systems show no palette). The
**current color** is shown in a box at the top of the palette — that's the color the
selected tool will draw with. Some tool options produce multi-colored effects.

You can extend your color choices via the **Apple Color Picker**: hold down **Option**
while clicking the current color. (Colors chosen from the Color Picker do not replace
the colors in the Kid Pix 2 palette.)

### Sound

Kid Pix 2 has a dazzling array of sound effects. Paint gurgles as it pours; letters
speak their names; firecrackers explode. With a microphone you can record spoken
messages to accompany your creations.

- **To turn off tool sounds,** choose "Tool Sounds" from the Goodies menu (choose it
  again to turn them back on).
- **To record a spoken message,** choose "Record" from the Goodies menu; to play back,
  choose "Play." (See Goodies menu.)

### Small Kids Mode

When in effect, the full menu bar disappears so there's no chance of opening a menu
accidentally. With Multifinder or System 7.0, a "bib" appears around the screen so the
user can't accidentally open a background application.

- **To turn on,** choose "Small Kids Mode" from the Goodies menu.
- **To temporarily view the full menu bar,** choose "Show Menu Bar" (it disappears again
  when drawing resumes).
- **To turn off,** choose "Show Menu Bar," then open Goodies and choose "Small Kids
  Mode" again.

> Note: You cannot open/save a file or quit while in Small Kids Mode — turn it off first.

### Saving and Retrieving Drawings

Kid Pix 2 saves drawings in **PICT** format and always gives you a chance to save before
starting a new drawing or quitting.

- **Save:** File ▸ Save, type a name, click OK.
- **Save As…:** File ▸ Save As…, type a new name (original remains under its old name).
- Recorded sounds are saved automatically with the drawing; deselect "Save with Sound"
  to save without sound (smaller files).
- **Open:** File ▸ Open, select from the list. Pictures saved with sound play it on
  opening (deselect "Open with Sound" to suppress).

### Starting a New Drawing

- **New:** File ▸ New (you'll be offered a chance to save first).
- You can also start fresh simply by erasing the screen with one of the eraser tools.

### Importing Pictures

You can import clip art, digitized photos, Photo CD files, Print Shop graphics, or other
paint-program pictures — anything in **PICT** format — via the Scrapbook desk accessory
or by opening the PICT file directly (File ▸ Open). Some cropping may occur so the
picture fits the drawing screen.

### Printing

Choose File ▸ Print, select a printout size from the four options and the number of
copies, then click Print. Click **Manual** to set up printer options yourself.

### Quitting

Choose File ▸ Quit (you'll be offered a chance to save). Turn off Small Kids Mode first
if it's on.

## About Kid Pix 2 Tools

> Tool options behave differently if you press **Option, Command, or Shift** during use.
> In the original manual these are marked with a star (★). Experiment to find out what
> they do.

### Wacky Pencil

Draws free-form lines. Select line widths, line styles, and line patterns from the
Options bar.

- The "wash" option produces a **tint** effect, like a watercolor wash — great for
  coloring "hidden pictures." On monochrome systems it produces a half-tone effect.
- The "rainbow" option **cycles through a color palette while it draws** (a palette of
  greys on monochrome systems).
- Two sets of line options give **square-** or **circle-shaped** lines.

### Line

Draws straight lines. Select line widths and patterns from the Options bar. One option
produces a **ray effect that inverts the color** of whatever it passes over.

- **Shift** while using draws perfect horizontal, vertical, or 45° lines.

### Rectangle ★

Draws rectangles in various patterns. First pattern = transparent; third pattern =
opaque white. One option produces a **rainbow rectangle**.

- **Option** while using draws a rectangle without a black border.
- **Shift** draws a perfect square.
- **Option + (rainbow)** fills a rectangle with shades of gray.

### Oval ★

Draws ovals in various patterns. First pattern = transparent; third = opaque white. One
option produces a **rainbow circle**.

- **Option** draws an oval without a black border.
- **Shift** draws a perfect circle.
- **Option + (rainbow)** fills a circle with shades of gray.

### Wacky Brush

Paints in a variety of ways depending on the tool option. There are **two sets** of
Wacky Brush options. On a color system most paint in the current color, but some are
multi-colored.

#### Wacky Brush Options

> Those marked ★ change when used with Option, Control, Shift, or Command.

- **Leaky Pen ★** — Your pen's sprung a leak. Ink soaks into your "drawing pad" whenever
  the pen stops moving.
- **Zig-Zag ★** — Bet you can't draw a straight line with this one!
- **Dots ★** — Lots and lots of dots.
- **Bubbly ★** — Soda pop time. Plenty of bubbles and fizz.
- **Pies ★** — Everyone's after a bigger slice of the pie.
- **Echoes ★** — Circles within circles within circles.
- **Northern Lights ★** — Colorful aurora borealis. Can be horizontal or vertical
  depending on which direction you move the brush.
- **Fuzzer ★** — "Fuzzes" whatever it moves over.
- **Magnifying Glass** — Enlarges whatever is under it. Move it around your drawing like
  Sherlock looking for a clue.
- **Spray Paint ★** — Sprays a line of mist.
- **Pine Needles** — Paints a trail of pine needles.
- **3-D ★** — Draws a line with a 3-D effect.
- **Kaleidoscope ★** — Kaleidoscope effect. Perfect symmetry wherever you move the brush.
- **Drippy Paint ★** — Messy paint drips as you draw.
- **Connect-The-Dots ★** — Make a connect-the-dots picture. Points are numbered as you
  draw; when you release the mouse the connecting line disappears.
  - **Option** while dragging keeps the line between dots.
  - Click the icon again to re-start the number sequence.
  - Click each dot individually to space dots farther apart (no connecting line appears).
- **ABC Alphabet Line ★** — The e. e. cummings effect. Paints with a line of alphabet
  letters or with words of your own creation. Enter your own text via "Alphabet Text" in
  the Goodies menu (or double-click the icon).
- **Swirl** — Lines swirl from the tip of the brush as you paint.
- **Inverter** — Inverts the color of everything it passes over.
- **Geometric** — Produces random geometrical drawings. Click to start.
  - **Option** while clicking makes a giant drawing.
- **Trees ★** — Generates "fractal" trees, each one different. Click to start a tree.
  - **Option** while clicking makes a giant tree with many branches.
  - **Command + Period** stops before the tree is finished.
- **Caterpillars ★** — Symmetrical caterpillars crawl around the screen.
- **Splatter Paint ★** — The Jackson Pollock (or cafeteria-after-a-food-fight) effect.
  - **Option** turns the circles into triangles.
  - **Command** makes the splatters larger.
  - **Shift** makes outlines.
- **The Looper ★** — Uh oh. Looks like the kitten's gotten into the yarn.
- **Roll-The-Dice ★** — Casts randomly numbered dice on your drawing.
- **Picture Brushes** — Paint with pictures:
  - A Galaxy of Stars ★
  - Lots of Hugs and X's ★
  - A Full Deck of Cards ★
  - Shapes and More Shapes ★

### Electric Mixer

**Transforms your drawing in a variety of crazy, mixed-up ways.**

> **To set mixer effects in motion, click the tool option you want, then click on your
> drawing.** (The original Mixer is click / click-repeatedly based — not drag-based.)

- **Night and Day** — Changes black to white and white to black. Inverts colors on a
  color system. Click to make the change; click again to reverse it.
- **Splash!** — Big blobs of paint are splashed on your drawing. The longer you hold the
  mouse button down, the more blobs appear.
- **Checkerboard** — Gives your drawing a checkerboard effect. Click to start the
  transformation; click again to get your original drawing back.
- **Wallpaper ★** — Makes multiple copies of a section of your drawing to create a tiled,
  or wallpaper, effect. The pattern changes depending on what the pointer moves over.
- **Venetian Blinds** — Slices your drawing into horizontal stripes. Click repeatedly to
  rearrange the pattern.
- **The Outliner** — Outlines every line in your drawing. Click repeatedly to create a
  series of fascinating effects.
- **Shadow Boxes** — Copies sections of your drawing and places them in overlapping
  boxes on the screen. Click repeatedly to produce more boxes.
- **Zoom In** — Zooms in on a portion of your drawing, making it **several times
  larger**. Click repeatedly to zoom in closer and closer.
- **Broken Glass** — **Shatters your drawing into many jagged pieces.**
- **Picture In A Picture ★** — Shrinks a copy of your picture. Hold down the mouse button
  to paste it anywhere.
- **The Highlighter** — **Highlights everything.**
- **Pattern Maker** — Turns your picture into a pattern.
- **Wrap Around ★** — Wraps your picture around from one end of the screen to the other.
  - **★ The Option key wraps the picture from top to bottom.**
- **Snowflakes And Rain Drops ★** — Snowflakes fall on your picture.
  - **Option** melts snowflakes into raindrops.

### Paint Can

Fills with the selected paint pattern.

### Eraser

Lets you erase all or part of your drawing in a variety of interesting ways.

#### Eraser Sizes

Erasers range from large to small — choose whichever size or shape you need.

#### Eraser Options

- **Firecracker ★** — Clear the screen in one big blast. Select, then click your drawing
  to start the explosive reaction.
  - **Option** freezes the Firecracker effect.
- **Hidden Pictures ★** — Kid Pix 2 has secrets. Uncover hidden pictures by selecting
  this option and erasing. Click the option again to uncover another hidden picture.
  - **Option** enlarges the erasing area.
- **White Circles** — Big white circles gobble up your drawing. Hold the mouse button
  until everything's gone.
- **Slip-Sliding Away** — Sliding doors erase your drawing.
- **#$%!** — An exclamatory end to it all!
- **Fade Away ★** — Watch your picture fade away.
- **Drop Out** — Chunks of your picture drop out until nothing is left.
- **Black Hole** — The black hole swallows up everything!
- **Count Down** — Counts down to today's date and time.
- **Sweep ★** — Spins and sweeps your picture away.

### Text ★

Place letters, numbers, and symbols wherever you want. When you select a letter, a voice
speaks its name in English or Spanish. Click to stamp the selected letter in place. If
one letter is stamped close to another, it aligns to the baseline of the previous letter.

- **Option** while stamping turns off the baseline feature.
- **"Type Text"** (Goodies menu) lets you enter text from the keyboard: position the
  pointer, click, and type. Change color or font before clicking again.

### Rubber Stamps ★

Place "rubber stamp" pictures on your drawing. **Eight sets** are available (multi-colored
on color systems). Click to stamp; hold and move the mouse to place many.

- **Option** while stamping doubles the size.
- **Shift** while stamping triples the size.
- **Option + Shift** makes gargantuan stamps. (Won't work on 9" monochrome monitors.)
- Edit/add stamps via "Edit Stamp" (Goodies) or double-click the Rubber Stamp icon.
- **Hint:** Move your mouse slowly to make a stamp brush.

### Moving Van ★

Moves a selected section of your drawing from one spot to another. Choose the size/shape,
position the van over the section, hold the mouse button down and move, then release.

- **Option** while moving moves a *copy* of the section.
- The adjustable-size ("marquee") option lets you capture and move any section, and you
  can cut, copy, clear, or paste it via the Edit menu.

### The Undo Guy

A friend in need. Click his anxious face and the results of your last action immediately
disappear. (Works the same as "Undo" on the Edit menu.)

## About Kid Pix 2 Menus

### File Menu

- **New (⌘N)** — Start a new file (you'll be offered a chance to save first).
- **Open** — Open a previously saved Kid Pix 2 file.
- **Close (⌘W)** — Close the current file.
- **Save (⌘S)** — Save a drawing.
- **Save As…** — Save the current drawing under a different name.
- **Print… (⌘P)** — Choose size and number of copies, then print.
- **Quit (⌘Q)** — Leave the program (you'll be offered a chance to save).

### Edit Menu

- **Undo (⌘Z)** — Undoes your last action.
- **Cut (⌘X)** — Cut a captured section to the Clipboard (use the Moving Van marquee to
  capture).
- **Copy (⌘C)** — Copy a captured section to the Clipboard.
- **Paste (⌘V)** — Paste the Clipboard onto your drawing (move the paste-jar pointer,
  then click to place).
- **Clear** — Remove a captured section (not placed on the Clipboard; Undo to get it
  back).

### Goodies Menu

- **Small Kids Mode (⌘K)** — Turn off the full menu bar; place a "bib" around the screen.
- **Edit Stamp… (⌘E)** — Edit the rubber-stamp pictures, or create a new one from
  scratch, in the **Stamp Editor** (Rotate, Flip, Undo, Clear, Color Palette, Restore
  Original, Cancel, OK).
- **Alphabet Text… (⌘A)** — Enter new letters/numbers/symbols for the Wacky Brush
  Alphabet Line.
- **Type Text (⌘T)** — Type text from your keyboard (or hold Option while clicking the
  Text tool).
- **Tool Sounds (⌘?)** — Turn Kid Pix 2 sound effects off/on.
- **Record Sound (⌘R)** — Record a voice or other sound message to accompany your
  drawing (requires a microphone or MacRecorder and System 6.0.7+).
- **Play Sound (⌘H)** — Play a recorded message attached to a drawing.

## Switcheroo Menu

- **Swap Stamps…** — Swap Rubber Stamp sets. (You can include stamps from all three sets
  in the same picture.)
- **Swap Hidden Pictures…** — Swap the Hidden Pictures used by the Hidden Pictures eraser.
- **DrawMe** — Start with a blank screen and a talking computer that suggests a fantasy
  scene to draw (via "Chaos Randomizing" — phrases freshly mixed each time, for thousands
  of suggestions). Replay the suggestion with Play Sound (⌘H).
- **ColorMe (⌘?)** — Electronic coloring-book pages. Open a ColorMe folder, pick a
  picture, and decorate it with paint tools and Rubber Stamps. Originals are never
  wrecked. Tip: use the Fill Bucket inside the lines; use the translucent Wacky Pencil to
  color over lines without erasing them.
- **Wacky TV…** — Play QuickTime movies in Kid Pix 2 with VCR-like controls and goofy
  special effects, and paste a movie into your picture.
- **Play Movie (⌘?)** — Play the pasted movie at any time.
- **Switch to SlideShow** — Switch to the SlideShow program (needs 3 MB available RAM).

### Wacky TV — Electric Mixer effects

The Electric Mixer in the Wacky TV does to a QuickTime movie the same kind of thing it
does to a Kid Pix 2 picture. Click it for **eight** zany effects:

- **Reverse!** — Play your movie backwards.
- **Stroboscope!** — The TV set goes on the fritz (but not really).
- **Zoomer!** — Zooms in on the movie. **Option-click** the Zoomer button to zoom out.
- **Speedo!** — Plays the movie super fast.
- **Splash!** — Lots of dancing dots scatter across the movie.
- **Shatter!** — The movie shatters like a piece of broken glass.
- **Searchlight!** — A roving square searches all over the movie.
- **Checkerboard!** — The movie breaks up into lots of little squares.

Click the Undo Guy to undo a special effect while it's playing. Click the **Paste Jar**
to make the TV go away and paste a freeze-frame of the movie into your picture.

## Let's Make a Slide Show

SlideShow is a separate program (launch via its icon or "Switch to SlideShow" in the
Switcheroo menu). Slides are carried by **Moving Vans** (99 in all), each holding a
picture/movie, a sound, a transition, and a display time. Vans are driven by Tom, the
Kid Pix 2 Dog.

### Filling a Moving Van

- **Pick a Picture!** (slide icon) — choose a picture or QuickTime movie for the van.
  Tip: keep all pictures for one show in the same folder.
- **Pick a Sound!** (musical note) — choose a ready-made mini-soundtrack, record your
  own, or pick "No sound." A filled note means a sound is attached.
- **Pick a Transition!** — a graphic way of going from one slide to the next (default is
  **Cut**). Options include Wipe Left, Panel Drop, **Venetian Blinds**, and more.
  Preview, then Select.
- **The Time Slider** — controls time between slides (default 3 s, range 0–30 s; auto-
  increases to match a longer sound). Drag it to adjust.

### The SlideShow Control Panel

- **Firecracker** — empty a selected Moving Van.
- **Undo Guy** — undo your last action (e.g., restore an emptied van).
- **Play once** / **Play looped** icons.
- Spacebar pauses; double-click (or ⌘-period) exits play.
- Press the mouse during a slide to jump to the Transition; press during a Transition to
  jump to the next slide.

### Editing & Saving

- Drag a full Moving Van to reorder; dropping it on another van nudges that van right.
- **Clean Up** (Goodies) discards empty vans (playback skips empties regardless).
- **Save As…** offers three formats:
  - **Normal** — needs the SlideShow application to run.
  - **StandAlone slide show** — a self-running disk you can give to others.
  - **QuickTime™ movie** — playable anywhere QuickTime is supported (including the Kid
    Pix 2 Wacky TV).
- **Animation tip:** make a sequence of slightly-changed pictures ("Cartoon 1", "Cartoon
  2", …), load them into vans with the **Cut** transition and **0-second** Time Slider,
  and play for a flip-book animation. Save as QuickTime and replay it in the Wacky TV —
  even faster.

## Kid Pix 2 Notes

- **Bigger color cells:** Set your monitor to "16 colors" in the Control Panel to make
  palette colors bigger (easier for small children to click).
- **Larger drawing screen:** Press the Spacebar while booting Kid Pix 2 to enlarge the
  drawing screen on monitors larger than 13". You may need to increase the application's
  memory (Get Info ▸ increase size by 500K+).
- **Memory:** Kid Pix 2 and SlideShow each need ~1464K free RAM for color systems.
- **QuickTime:** requires a 68020+ processor, 32-bit Color QuickDraw, and System 6.0.7+.
  Won't run on an SE or Plus.
- **SlideShow** works in 1-bit, 4-bit, or 8-bit monitor modes; open a show in the same
  mode it was created. StandAlone shows play in any mode.
